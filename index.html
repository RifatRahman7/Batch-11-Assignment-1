Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh has seen remarkable strides in innovation across various sectors in recent years. The country's progress in technology, social entrepreneurship.

Sustainability

Sustainability has become a key focus in Bangladesh's development efforts, especially given the country’s vulnerability to environmental risks like climate change.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh’s infrastructure has seen significant development in recent years, driven by rapid urbanization, population growth, and a focus on economic modernization.

Empowerment

Empowerment is a key concept that is closely tied to social, economic, and political progress. It refers to the process through which individuals or groups gain control over their lives.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"I believe we can create a world where poverty no longer exists, where the resources of the world are used to benefit people rather than to exploit them, and where the well-being of people becomes the purpose of business, not just profit. Bangladesh has proven that it is possible to create a society that is both economically sustainable and socially inclusive. The concept of social business is a step toward a world where people’s needs and aspirations are met by the systems that serve them, not by creating more inequality and suffering. We need to create new ways of thinking, and new systems that will replace the old ones. We must be able to think beyond profit and understand that human happiness and fulfillment must take precedence over material gain."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
